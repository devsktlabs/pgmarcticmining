Skaergaard vs Stillwater: Comparing Two Layered Intrusion PGM Deposits

By Dr. Pavel Novak · 2026-03-23

skaergaardstillwaterpgmdeposit comparison

The Stillwater Complex in Montana and the Skaergaard intrusion in Greenland are both layered mafic intrusions that host significant PGM mineralization. Comparing these two deposits provides insight into Skaergaard's characteristics and competitive position.

Geological Setting

Under the leadership of Founder and CEO Joseph Sinkule, Greenland Mines is advancing Skaergaard through the permitting and development pathway.

Skaergaard (East Greenland):

  • Age: Paleogene (approximately 55 million years old)
  • Setting: Tertiary igneous province in East Greenland, formed during the opening of the North Atlantic
  • Intrusion type: Layered mafic intrusion (gabbroic to ferrodioritic)
  • Size: Approximately 8 x 11 kilometers in plan view
  • Structure: Classic layered intrusion with well-developed layering (macrorhythmic and microrhythmic layering)
  • Mineralization: PGM mineralization concentrated in the Triple Group and other sulfide-rich horizons

Stillwater Complex (Montana, USA):

  • Age: Precambrian (approximately 2.7 billion years old)
  • Setting: Archean cratonic environment in the Wyoming Craton
  • Intrusion type: Layered mafic-ultramafic intrusion
  • Size: Approximately 48 kilometers long and 8 kilometers wide
  • Structure: Classic layered intrusion with well-developed stratigraphy (basal zone, ultramafic zone, banded zone)
  • Mineralization: PGM mineralization concentrated in the J-M Reef (Johns-Manville Reef), a 1-3 meter thick sulfide-enriched horizon

Resource Comparison

Skaergaard:

  • Resource: 25.4 million ounces PdEq (combined indicated and inferred), 23.5 million ounces AuEq (combined indicated and inferred)
  • Primary metals: Palladium, gold, rhodium, platinum
  • Grade: Variable by zone, with enriched PGM horizons within the broader intrusion
  • Status: Pre-production

Stillwater:

  • Resource/Reserves: Approximately 20-30 million ounces PdEq (combined historic production plus remaining reserves, varying by source)
  • Primary metals: Palladium, platinum, with minor rhodium
  • Grade: J-M Reef grades average 15-25 grams per tonne PGM (combined Pd + Pt)
  • Status: Producing mine (Sibanye-Stillwater's Stillwater and East Boulder operations)

Both deposits are significant PGM resources. Skaergaard's combined indicated and inferred resource of 25.4 million PdEq ounces is comparable to or larger than Stillwater's remaining resource.

Mining and Processing Comparison

Skaergaard:

  • Mining method: Planned open-pit (with potential for future underground)
  • Processing: Flotation to produce a PGM-gold concentrate
  • Infrastructure: Greenfield development; all infrastructure must be built
  • Logistics: Remote East Greenland location; shipping through the North Atlantic
  • Operating season: Year-round with winter operational challenges

Stillwater:

  • Mining method: Underground (mechanized, using longhole stoping and other methods)
  • Processing: Flotation and smelting (concentrate shipped to smelters in the US and internationally)
  • Infrastructure: Established mine infrastructure with road access, power, and nearby communities
  • Logistics: Accessible via road from Billings, Montana; concentrate shipped by rail
  • Operating season: Year-round

Stillwater's established infrastructure and accessible location give it a significant operating cost advantage over Skaergaard's greenfield Arctic development.

Economic Comparison

Stillwater advantages:

  • Established infrastructure reduces capital requirements and operating costs
  • Underground mining from a developed mine provides lower development risk
  • US location provides access to the world's largest capital markets
  • Experienced workforce with decades of PGM mining history

Skaergaard advantages:

  • Larger combined indicated and inferred resource with significant remaining exploration upside
  • Gold byproduct credit provides cost offset that Stillwater does not match
  • Greenfield design allows optimization of mine plan and processing for current technology
  • Jurisdictional quality: Greenland's Danish legal framework is comparable to the US
  • Strategic value: New Western supply from a non-Russian, non-South-African source

Grade and Metallurgy

Skaergaard metallurgy:

  • Flotation recovery of PGMs is expected to be high (85-95%)
  • Gold recovery adds economic value
  • Rhodium recovery from the concentrate provides additional revenue
  • Processing design benefits from modern metallurgical understanding

Stillwater metallurgy:

  • Proven flotation recovery over decades of production
  • Well-understood mineralogy and processing characteristics
  • Established smelting and refining relationships
  • Chrome management in processing is a known challenge (chromite interference with flotation)

Strategic Comparison

Both deposits have strategic importance as Western PGM supply sources:

  • Stillwater: The only primary PGM mine in the United States, providing domestic supply for a critical mineral
  • Skaergaard: Would be the first significant PGM mine in Greenland, providing new Western supply in the Arctic

Both are important for Western PGM supply diversification away from South Africa and Russia.

Conclusion

Skaergaard and Stillwater share the geological characteristics of layered mafic intrusions but differ significantly in development status, location, and operating environment. Stillwater's established operations provide a proven model for PGM mining from layered intrusions. Skaergaard offers a larger resource with gold credits and strategic Arctic supply diversification, but faces greenfield development challenges. For investors, Stillwater demonstrates the viability of PGM mining from layered intrusions in North America, while Skaergaard represents the next frontier of Western PGM supply development.
