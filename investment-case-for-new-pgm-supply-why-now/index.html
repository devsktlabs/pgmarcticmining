Investment Case for New PGM Supply: Why Now

By Dr. Pavel Novak · 2026-03-23

pgminvestmentnew supplyskaergaardmining finance

The investment case for new PGM supply has rarely been stronger than it is in 2026. Declining South African production, constrained Russian supply, growing Western strategic interest, and favorable metal prices create a convergence of factors that make new PGM development projects like Skaergaard attractive to a broad range of investors.

The Supply Deficit as Investment Thesis

The PGM supply deficit is not a temporary market dislocation. It is a structural trend driven by fundamental geological and operational constraints:

  • **South African decline**: The Bushveld Complex, source of 70-75% of global PGM supply, is in irreversible decline. Mines are getting deeper, grades are falling, costs are rising, and production is dropping by 1-3% per year
  • **Russian constraints**: Sanctions, market access difficulties, and operational challenges constrain Norilsk Nickel's production
  • **Recycling limitations**: Recycling provides 25-30% of supply but faces fleet turnover constraints and cannot grow fast enough to fill the gap
  • **Limited new projects**: Very few new PGM projects are in the development pipeline globally

    • This structural deficit means that the PGM market needs new primary supply, and the economics of new supply are increasingly favorable.

    The Strategic Premium

    New PGM supply from Western jurisdictions commands a strategic premium:

  • **Government support**: The EU CRMA, US critical mineral policies, and Nordic government programs provide policy support and potential funding for Western PGM projects
  • **Institutional demand**: ESG-focused institutional investors are actively seeking exposure to Western critical mineral supply, creating a growing investor base
  • **Industrial customer interest**: Automakers and industrial consumers of PGMs are seeking to diversify supply chains away from SA and Russia, creating potential for offtake agreements and strategic partnerships
  • **First-mover advantage**: Skaergaard would be the first significant PGM mine in Greenland, establishing a precedent for Arctic PGM supply development

    • Risk-Return Profile

    New PGM development projects like Skaergaard offer a distinctive risk-return profile:

    Risks:

  • Development risk: Greenfield mine development faces geological, engineering, and execution risk
  • Permittting risk: Greenland's EIA process, while well-defined, adds time and uncertainty
  • Construction risk: Arctic construction faces weather, logistics, and cost overrun risk
  • Commodity price risk: PGM and gold prices could decline, reducing project economics
  • Financing risk: Project financing for a first-in-jurisdiction Arctic mine may be challenging

    • Returns:

  • Scale upside: Skaergaard's 25.4M oz PdEq resource could support decades of production
  • Multi-metal diversification: Palladium + gold + rhodium exposure reduces single-metal risk
  • Jurisdictional premium: Greenland's Danish legal framework supports valuation premiums
  • Strategic premium: Western supply diversification creates additional value for strategic investors
  • Exploration upside: Underground potential and regional exploration upside could add resource beyond the current estimate

    • Comparing Development-Stage PGM Investments

    For investors evaluating Skaergaard against other development-stage PGM investments:

  • **Ivanhoe Platreef**: Larger but still in South Africa (jurisdictional discount), nickel and copper co-product credits, advanced development stage but construction delayed
  • **Various Canadian PGM exploration**: Earlier stage with smaller resources, Canadian jurisdiction (positive), but uncertain development timeline
  • **Skaergaard**: Greenland jurisdiction (new but high-quality), multi-metal credits (Pd + Au + Rh), open-pit (lower technical risk), pre-feasibility stage

    • Skaergaard's combination of jurisdictional quality, multi-metal credits, and open-pit development makes it one of the more attractive development-stage PGM opportunities available.

    The Timing Argument

    Timing matters in mining investment. Several factors suggest that now is the right time for PGM supply investment:

  • **Post-correction valuations**: PGM company valuations declined during the 2022-2024 price correction, creating attractive entry points
  • **Policy momentum**: Western critical mineral policies are providing unprecedented support for domestic and allied-country PGM development
  • **Supply gap widening**: The PGM supply deficit is widening, and the timeline to develop new mines (8-12 years) means that projects initiated now will produce into a supply-constrained market
  • **Gold price tailwind**: Record gold prices enhance the economics of PGM projects with gold byproduct credits

    • Capital Requirements and Funding

    Skaergaard will require significant capital for development:

  • **Pre-production capital**: Estimated US$800 million to US$1.5 billion for mine construction, processing plant, infrastructure, and port facility
  • **Funding options**: Equity issuance, project finance (debt), royalty and streaming, government grants, strategic partner investment
  • **Funding timeline**: Capital will be raised in stages aligned with project milestones (feasibility study completion, EIA approval, construction decision)

    • The scale of capital required means that Skaergaard's development will likely involve a combination of funding sources, including potential strategic investment from PGM consumers or governments.

    Conclusion

    The investment case for new PGM supply is supported by the structural supply deficit, strategic premium for Western supply, and favorable metal price environment. Skaergaard combines jurisdictional quality, multi-metal diversification, and open-pit development in a single investment. While development-stage mining carries significant risk, the convergence of supply deficit, policy support, and strategic demand creates an environment where the potential returns are commensurate with the risks. For investors seeking PGM exposure with strategic Western supply diversification, Skaergaard is one of the most compelling opportunities available.
