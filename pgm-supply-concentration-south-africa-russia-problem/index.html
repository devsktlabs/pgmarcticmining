PGM Supply Concentration: The South Africa and Russia Problem

By Dr. Pavel Novak · 2026-03-23

pgm supplysouth africarussiadiversification

The global PGM supply chain is one of the most geographically concentrated of any major commodity. Approximately 80-85% of palladium supply and 70-75% of platinum supply comes from just two countries: South Africa and Russia. This concentration creates significant supply chain risk that Western governments and industrial consumers are increasingly eager to address.

The Concentration by the Numbers

Palladium supply sources:

  • South Africa: ~70-75% (Bushveld Complex: Amplats, Implats, Sibanye-Stillwater, Northam, etc.)
  • Russia: ~10-12% (Norilsk Nickel)
  • North America: ~5% (Stillwater Complex, Montana; Sudbury Basin, Canada)
  • Recycling: ~25-30% (global, from catalytic converters and industrial sources)
  • Other: <3% (Zimbabwe, other minor producers)

Platinum supply sources:

  • South Africa: ~70-75% (Bushveld Complex)
  • Russia: ~10-12% (Norilsk Nickel)
  • Zimbabwe: ~3-5% (Great Dyke: Zimplats, Mimosa, Unki)
  • North America: ~2-3% (Stillwater, Sudbury)
  • Recycling: ~25-30%
  • Other: <2%

The concentration is even more extreme for rhodium, with South Africa alone supplying approximately 80-85% of global production.

South Africa: The Risk Profile

South Africa's PGM sector faces multiple structural challenges:

  • Declining grades: The Bushveld Complex mines are getting deeper and lower-grade. Merensky and UG2 reef grades have declined at many operations, reducing output per tonne mined
  • Energy crisis: Eskom, South Africa's state utility, has implemented rolling blackouts (load-shedding) for years, forcing mines to reduce operations or invest in expensive backup power
  • Labor instability: Wage negotiations, safety stoppages (SA has some of the world's deepest and most dangerous mines), and occasional strikes disrupt operations
  • Infrastructure: Rail, port, and road infrastructure in the mining regions is deteriorating, increasing logistics costs and delays
  • Governance: Corruption, regulatory uncertainty, and policy changes (including discussions of nationalization in the past) create political risk
  • Cost inflation: Labor, energy, and consumable costs in South Africa have risen faster than many other mining jurisdictions

The trajectory is clear: South African PGM production peaked in the 2006-2010 period and has been declining since. Most forecasts project continued decline at 1-3% per year.

Russia: The Sanctions Risk

Russia's PGM supply from Norilsk Nickel faces geopolitical risk:

  • Sanctions: Western sanctions imposed after the 2022 Ukraine invasion constrain Norilsk's access to technology, equipment, financing, and Western markets. While PGMs have not been directly sanctioned, the broader sanctions environment creates operational constraints
  • Market access: Russian PGMs must find buyers through non-Western intermediaries, primarily in China and India, creating supply chain complexity and price discounts
  • Operational risk: Norilsk's Arctic operations face their own challenges (infrastructure, environmental compliance, labor)
  • Geopolitical leverage: Russia has historically used energy and resource supply as geopolitical leverage. Dependence on Russian PGMs creates vulnerability for Western industrial consumers

The Diversification Imperative

The supply concentration creates strategic risk for Western nations:

  • Automotive industry: European, North American, and Japanese automakers depend on PGM supply chains that run through South Africa and Russia. Any disruption to these supply chains could halt vehicle production
  • Defense: PGMs are used in defense applications including catalytic converters for military vehicles, chemical catalysts for explosives production, and electronics
  • Energy transition: PGMs are essential for hydrogen fuel cells and electrolyzers, key technologies in the energy transition
  • Economic security: Concentration of critical mineral supply in two countries, one of which is a strategic rival, creates economic security vulnerability

Policy Responses

Western governments have responded to PGM supply concentration:

  • Critical mineral designations: PGMs are designated as critical minerals by the EU, US, Japan, and other Western governments
  • Stockpiling: Some nations have established or expanded PGM strategic stockpiles
  • Supply chain diversification programs: Government programs encourage domestic PGM exploration and development
  • IRA incentives: The US Inflation Reduction Act includes incentives for domestic critical mineral production and supply chain development
  • EU Critical Raw Materials Act: Sets targets for domestic and allied-country supply of critical raw materials

Skaergaard as a Diversification Play

Skaergaard directly addresses the PGM supply concentration problem:

  • Scale: 25.4 million PdEq ounces is large enough to make a meaningful supply contribution

Other Diversification Projects

Skaergaard is not the only project addressing PGM supply concentration, but it is among the most significant:

  • Ivanhoe Platreef (South Africa): Large PGM-nickel-copper project, but still in South Africa, so does not solve jurisdictional concentration
  • Waterberg (Platinum Group Metals, South Africa): Another large Bushveld project, same jurisdictional concentration
  • Stillwater expansion (USA): Potential expansion of existing operations, but limited growth potential
  • Various exploration projects: Early-stage exploration in Canada, Finland, and other jurisdictions, but most are years from production

Skaergaard stands out as a new-jurisdiction project with sufficient scale to matter.

Conclusion

PGM supply concentration in South Africa and Russia creates strategic risk for Western industrial consumers and governments. The concentration is worsening as SA production declines and Russian supply faces geopolitical constraints. Skaergaard offers new Western supply diversification for a critical mineral, directly addressing a strategic vulnerability. For investors, Skaergaard's jurisdictional diversification premium is a real and growing factor in its investment thesis.
